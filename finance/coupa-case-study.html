How a SaaS company prioritized growth over profitability ‚Äî and why the math worked

üè¢ Company Overview

Coupa Software is a SaaS platform for Business Spend Management (BSM) ‚Äî helping companies manage indirect spending (everything that isn't core COGS). Think procurement, invoicing, expenses, and supplier management.

Led by CEO Rob Bernshteyn, who joined in 2009 and transformed the company from on-premise software to a cloud-first SaaS model. Bernshteyn brought deep enterprise SaaS experience, having previously been VP of Marketing at SuccessFactors.

1. Profitability

Gross margins of ~52% are healthy for SaaS, but the company was losing ~$12M/year. This is deliberate ‚Äî the losses came from aggressive sales & marketing investment, not from a broken business model. Each dollar spent on growth generated compounding future revenue.

2. Asset Intensity

Coupa operated with negative net operating assets (~-30% of revenue). How? Deferred revenue. Customers pay upfront for annual subscriptions, so the business is effectively funded by its customers. This is the SaaS dream ‚Äî you get paid before you deliver the service.

3. Growth (The Key Driver)

This is where the math gets compelling:

üí° Key Takeaway

For high-growth ventures, the priority is NOT immediate profitability. When your unit economics are attractive (each sales rep generates >110% IRR), the rational strategy is to invest aggressively in growth. The "losses" aren't losses ‚Äî they're investments with compounding returns. You're buying future cash flows at a discount.
