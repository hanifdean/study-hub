# Indonesian Equity Watchlist — Source Data for Deep Dive Pages

## 1. ADRO — Alamtri Resources Indonesia (formerly Adaro Energy)
- **Ticker:** ADRO.JK
- **Type:** Holdco / Diversified Energy & Mining
- **Post spin-off:** Renamed from Adaro Energy to Alamtri Resources after spinning off thermal coal (AADI)
- **Key holdings:**
  - ~83.8% stake in ADMR (Adaro Minerals — met coal + aluminium)
  - Adaro Green (renewable energy — PLTA Mentarang 1,375 MW hydro, target COD 2030)
- **Controlling shareholders:** PT Adaro Strategic + Garibaldi Thohir (via SRTG)
- **Strategy:** Transform to 50% non-coal revenue by 2030. Focus on hilirisasi (downstream), green energy, metalurgy
- **Financials (Samuel est):** Net profit: 2022 $2.49B → 2023 $1.64B → 2024F $1.58B → 2025F $504M → 2026F decline post spin-off
- **Key risks:** Holdco discount (typically 20-40% in Indonesia), execution on green projects, loss of AADI cash cow
- **Investment thesis:** Sum-of-parts play. ADMR stake + Adaro Green optionality. Buy if holdco discount too wide.

## 2. AADI — Adaro Andalan Indonesia
- **Ticker:** AADI.JK
- **Type:** Pure thermal coal miner (spun off from ADRO)
- **IPO:** Late 2024/early 2025 via spin-off mechanism
- **Business:** Integrated thermal coal — mining, logistics, power generation
- **Financials (9M 2025):**
  - Revenue: $3.6B (down 11% YoY due to lower coal prices)
  - Net profit: $655M (down 44% YoY)
  - Coal sales: 52.69 million tons (on track for FY target 65-67M tons)
  - Stripping ratio: 4.2x (guidance 4.3x)
- **Dividend:** Paid $250M interim dividend (~42.57% of 9M net profit). Targeting 45% DPR. Est. yield >10%
- **Markets:** Indonesia (largest), Malaysia, India, China — dominated by coal-fired power plants
- **Key value:** High dividend yield play, mature cash-flow business, proven operations
- **Risks:** Thermal coal price volatility, ESG headwinds, long-term demand shift to renewables

## 3. ADMR — Adaro Minerals Indonesia (now Alamtri Minerals)
- **Ticker:** ADMR.JK
- **Type:** Met coal + aluminium smelter
- **Parent:** ~83.8% owned by ADRO
- **Business segments:**
  - Metallurgical coal mining (5 CCoW concessions in East & Central Kalimantan)
  - Aluminium smelter (PT Kalimantan Aluminium Industry / KAI) in North Kalimantan
- **Financials (9M 2025):**
  - Revenue: Rp11.26 trillion
  - Net profit: Rp3.41 trillion
  - Met coal production surged 30% in FY2024
- **Smelter progress:** Phased operations targeted by end of 2025 / early 2026
- **Growth driver:** Green aluminium (powered by PLTA Mentarang hydro) — potential 1.5M tpa capacity
- **Key value:** High-growth play. Met coal demand from steel industry + aluminium smelter optionality
- **Risks:** Smelter execution, met coal price cycles, capex-heavy phase

## 4. ELSA — Elnusa
- **Ticker:** ELSA.JK
- **Type:** Oil & gas services (Pertamina subsidiary)
- **Founded:** 1969 (as PT Electronika Nusantara)
- **Majority shareholder:** PT Pertamina (via subsidiaries)
- **Business segments:**
  1. Upstream oil & gas services (seismic, drilling, well services)
  2. Energy distribution & logistics (fuel distribution, trading)
  3. Supporting services (IT, fleet management, maintenance)
- **Financials (9M 2025):**
  - Revenue: Rp10.48 trillion (+8.6% YoY)
  - Net profit: Rp526.6 billion (down 4.5% YoY from Rp551.2B)
  - EPS: Rp72.13
- **Key value:** Stable Pertamina-linked business, steady cash flow, defensive play in energy sector
- **Growth:** Still growing revenue positively despite oil price pressure
- **Risks:** Oil price dependency, Pertamina contract concentration, limited upside vs. pure-play E&P

## 5. SRTG — Saratoga Investama Sedaya
- **Ticker:** SRTG.JK
- **Type:** Investment holding company (Indonesia's premier listed PE/VC holdco)
- **Key portfolio holdings (approx stakes):**
  - ADRO/AADI: ~15.2% (23% of NAV)
  - TBIG (Tower Bersama Infrastructure): ~31.6% (29% of NAV)
  - MDKA (Merdeka Copper Gold): ~19.1% (15% of NAV)
  - MPMX (Mitra Pinasthika Mustika): ~56.7% (5% of NAV)
  - Plus other unlisted/early-stage investments
- **NAV:** Rp53.9 trillion (2024), +10.5% growth YoY
- **Dividend income:** ~Rp1.7T (9M 2024), expected Rp2.4T FY 2025-26
- **Key value:** Diversified exposure to Indonesia's best conglomerates at a holdco discount. NAV growth + dividend income.
- **Holdco discount:** Typically trades at 40-60% discount to NAV
- **Founders:** Edwin Soeryadjaya, Sandiaga Uno (former VP candidate)
- **Risks:** Deep holdco discount may never close, concentrated in resources/infra, governance perception

## 6. MDKA — Merdeka Copper Gold
- **Ticker:** MDKA.JK
- **Type:** Diversified mining (gold, copper, nickel, battery materials)
- **Key assets:**
  - Tujuh Bukit Gold Mine (East Java) — producing
  - Tujuh Bukit Copper Project (underground, under development)
  - Wetar Copper Mine — producing
  - Pani Gold Project (via EMAS, ~79k oz target 2026, ramp to 500k oz by 2032)
  - Nickel (via MBMA — PT Merdeka Battery Materials)
- **Financials (FY 2025):**
  - Gold production: 103,156 oz (TB Gold)
  - Gold sales: 104,168 oz at avg $3,138/oz (+32% YoY)
  - Copper: 10,454 tons (Wetar)
  - Nickel saprolite: 7.0M wmt (+42% YoY)
  - Nickel limonite: 14.7M wmt (+45% YoY)
  - Revenue: Rp31.69 trillion
  - Cash: $364M + $460M undrawn facilities (as of 1H25)
- **2026 targets:**
  - Gold: 180,000-205,000 oz (Pani + Tujuh Bukit). HUGE growth from 103k
  - Nickel: 8-10M wmt saprolite, 20-25M wmt limonite
- **Key value:** Multi-commodity growth story. Pani Gold is transformational. Nickel for battery supply chain.
- **Risks:** Execution on Pani, nickel price pressure, complex corporate structure (MBMA, EMAS subsidiaries)

## 7. MEDC — Medco Energi Internasional
- **Ticker:** MEDC.JK
- **Type:** Integrated energy (oil & gas E&P + power generation + mining stake)
- **Key assets:**
  - Oil & gas: Multiple PSC blocks (Corridor, Rimau, B Natuna, South Sumatra, Oman blocks 60 & 48)
  - Power: Medco Power (thermal + renewables — IPPs in Ijen, Bali Timur)
  - Mining: ~20.92% stake in AMMN (Amman Mineral — gold & copper)
- **Financials (2025):**
  - Production: 156,000 mboepd
  - Electricity sales: 4,371 GWh
  - Revenue: gas ~70% of total, oil ~30%
  - Profit plunged 81% in 1H25 (partly due to AMMN operating loss of $31M)
- **2026 targets:**
  - Production: 165,000-170,000 mboepd (RECORD HIGH)
  - Electricity: 4,550 GWh, 24% renewable
- **AMMN contribution:** Est. AMMN net profit $1.7B in 2026 → ~$360M contribution to MEDC (Indo Premier est.)
- **Key value:** Gas-heavy portfolio (more stable than oil), AMMN optionality, record production targets, renewable expansion
- **Analyst target:** Rp2,000 (Indo Premier)
- **Risks:** Oil price volatility, AMMN phase transition (lower output 2025), capex needs, Oman execution

## 8. EMAS — Merdeka Gold Resources
- **Ticker:** EMAS.JK
- **Type:** Pre-production gold miner (subsidiary of MDKA)
- **IPO:** Sep 2025, raised $281M (Indonesia's largest IPO of 2025)
- **Key asset:** Pani Gold Project (Sulawesi)
  - Processing capacity: up to 19.0 Mtpa
  - Construction ~50% complete as of mid-2025, heap leach facility completion end 2025
  - First gold production: Q1 2026
  - Target: ~79k oz in 2026, ramp to 500k oz by 2032
  - Multi-decade mine life
- **Financials:** Still pre-revenue. Accumulated losses $9.21M (as of Q1 2025). Negative operating cash flow.
- **Valuation at IPO:** $3.2B. IPO price range Rp1,800-3,020. PBV 4.0-5.3x (above sector avg)
- **Key value:** Pure optionality on one of Indonesia's largest undeveloped gold projects. Massive production ramp.
- **Risks:** Execution (construction delays), no revenue yet, high valuation, funding needs, gold price dependency
