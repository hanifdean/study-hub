EUV mask inspection monopoly. Lasertec is the only company capable of inspecting EUV photomasks at production throughput. Without Lasertec, EUV lithography doesn't work.
"Japan's ASML." Just as ASML monopolizes EUV lithography, Lasertec monopolizes EUV mask inspection ‚Äî a symbiotic relationship tied to advanced node adoption.
~100% market share. No viable competitor exists for actinic (EUV-wavelength) mask inspection. KLA competes in DUV inspection but not EUV.
ACTIS A300 launch. Next-generation EUV mask inspection system released March 2024, supporting High-NA EUV roadmap.

üè¢ Company Overview

Lasertec Corporation, founded in 1960, is headquartered in Yokohama, Japan. Originally a small semiconductor equipment supplier, the company transformed into a critical EUV enabler by developing actinic (EUV-wavelength) photomask inspection systems. Every leading-edge fab (TSMC, Samsung, Intel) and mask shop must use Lasertec equipment to produce defect-free EUV masks.

üêÇ Bull Case

  • True monopoly: ~100% share with no credible competitor developing EUV inspection
  • ASML symbiosis: Every ASML EUV tool shipped requires Lasertec mask inspection
  • High-NA EUV opportunity: Next-gen lithography creates new inspection requirements
  • Growing mask complexity: Multi-patterning increases inspection passes per mask
  • Recurring revenue: Service contracts and consumables from installed base

üêª Bear Case

  • Short-seller scrutiny: Scorpion Capital report questioned accounting and valuation
  • Customer concentration: ~3-4 major customers (TSMC, Samsung, Intel mask shops)
  • Small TAM: EUV mask inspection is a niche market; limited total addressable market
  • Technology risk: Alternative inspection methods or reduced mask complexity could emerge
  • Valuation volatility: High-multiple stock sensitive to any demand slowdown
