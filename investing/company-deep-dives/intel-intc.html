Foundry pivot underway. Intel is the world's largest IDM attempting to become a credible #2 foundry behind TSMC. The 18A node has reached HVM at Fab 52 in Arizona.
Strategic validation. Microsoft reportedly committed to 18A-P for Maia 2 AI accelerators. NVIDIA invested $5B equity, SoftBank $2B in IFS. ~$8B CHIPS Act funding received.

üéØ Variant View - Where We Disagree with Consensus

What consensus believes:
  • Intel is a "Hold" - the turnaround is too uncertain to bet on.
  • x86 is in structural decline vs. ARM and custom silicon.
  • 18A will never achieve TSMC-equivalent yields and reliability.
  • $15-20B/year capex will destroy ROIC for years.
Why we think consensus is wrong:
  1. 18A HVM is real, not vaporware. Intel reached high-volume manufacturing at Fab 52 with ~40K wafers/month. The PDK has been delivered to customers. This is execution, not a roadmap promise. The skepticism is priced in; the delivery isn't.
  2. Microsoft anchor validates external credibility. Microsoft's reported commitment to 18A-P for Maia 2 is the first major external customer for Intel Foundry. One major hyperscaler opens doors to others. This changes the narrative from "can Intel compete?" to "Intel is competing."
  3. AI server demand supports x86 near-term. Custom Xeon with NVLink integration validates Intel CPUs in AI nodes. AI servers still need x86 control planes. The ARM threat is real long-term but not immediate.
The evidence:
  • Q4 2025 earnings: 18A in HVM at Fab 52, 18A-P PDK delivered to customers.
  • Reports: Microsoft using 18A-P for Maia 2 AI accelerators (potentially $15B lifetime deal value).
  • DCAI revenue: $4.7B in Q4 (+9% YoY) with Granite Rapids ramp underway.
  • Strategic investments: $5B NVIDIA equity, $2B SoftBank, ~$8B CHIPS Act - external validation of the turnaround.

‚ò†Ô∏è Kill Conditions ‚Äî When to Exit

Kill #1: 18A external customer deals fail to materialize by Q4 2026

The foundry thesis requires external validation. If Microsoft deal doesn't close AND no other major customer is announced by Q4 2026, the foundry pivot has failed. One or two quarters of delay is acceptable; a full year without external revenue is fatal. Exit price: $30.

Kill #2: Server CPU share drops below 55%

Currently ~72% server share. AMD EPYC and ARM are gaining. If share drops below 55%, pricing power collapses and the CPU cash cow thesis breaks. Watch quarterly share data from Mercury Research. Exit price: $35.

Kill #3: Foundry losses exceed $6B/year for 3+ years

Intel Foundry currently loses ~$5B/year. If losses increase and persist for 3+ years without external revenue traction, the capital destruction is unsustainable. Monitor quarterly segment reporting. Exit price: $28.

Kill #4: 14A development significantly delayed

18A needs 14A to maintain technology leadership. If 14A slips by >1 year from current roadmap, Intel falls permanently behind TSMC. Watch technology announcements and Investor Day updates. Exit price: $32.

Kill #5: CEO change before turnaround proves out

Third CEO in 3 years. If Lip-Bu Tan leaves or is replaced before 2027, the turnaround strategy lacks continuity. Leadership stability is essential for multi-year execution. Re-evaluate position entirely.
