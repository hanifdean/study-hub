üìñ Jargon Decoder

EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet Lithography) ‚Ü© back

The cutting-edge lithography technology using 13.5nm light to print the smallest transistor features (5nm, 3nm, 2nm). ASML has a 100% monopoly on EUV machines.

DUV (Deep Ultraviolet Lithography) ‚Ü© back

The previous generation lithography using 193nm light. Can reach ~7nm with multi-patterning tricks, but cannot go smaller. Still widely used for non-cutting-edge chips.

Photoresist ‚Ü© back

Light-sensitive chemical coatings applied to silicon wafers. When exposed to UV light through a mask, they create the circuit patterns. Japan controls ~90% of advanced photoresist production.

Silicon Wafer ‚Ü© back

A thin, polished disc of ultra-pure silicon (300mm/12" diameter) used as the base substrate for manufacturing chips.

Photomask (Reticle) ‚Ü© back

A glass plate containing the circuit pattern that is projected onto the wafer during lithography. Like a stencil for chip patterns.

Czochralski Process ‚Ü© back

The method used to grow single-crystal silicon ingots by slowly pulling a seed crystal from a crucible of molten silicon.

CMP (Chemical Mechanical Polishing) ‚Ü© back

A process that makes silicon wafers mirror-smooth using a combination of chemical slurry and mechanical abrasion.

Rare Earth Elements ‚Ü© back

A group of 17 metallic elements critical for semiconductors, magnets, and defense applications. China controls ~90% of global refining.

üìå Worth Knowing

  • Why can't anyone else build EUV? ‚Äî ASML spent 20+ years and invested over $10 billion developing EUV technology. The machine requires components from ~800 suppliers across multiple countries (Zeiss for optics, Cymer/TRUMPF for the light source). No single country, let alone company, could replicate this from scratch. China has been trying ‚Äî and is still decades behind.
  • The neon gas crisis ‚Äî Ukraine produced ~50% of the world's semiconductor-grade neon (purified from Russian industrial output). When the Russia-Ukraine war started in 2022, prices spiked 10√ó. Chip companies have since diversified, but it showed how fragile the supply chain is for materials most people have never heard of.
  • Photoresists ‚Äî Japan's hidden leverage ‚Äî Japan controls ~90% of advanced photoresist production (the light-sensitive coatings used in lithography). Companies like JSR, TOK, and Shin-Etsu Chemical are essentially irreplaceable. Japan briefly restricted exports to South Korea in 2019, showing this isn't just theoretical leverage.
  • The "weaponization" of the supply chain ‚Äî Every major player in the semiconductor supply chain is now thinking about how their products can be used as geopolitical leverage. The US restricts chips & equipment ‚Üí China restricts rare earths ‚Üí Japan has photoresist leverage ‚Üí Taiwan (TSMC) controls manufacturing. It's a multipolar standoff where everyone has something to threaten with.
  • Why smaller nodes need EUV ‚Äî Older DUV (deep ultraviolet) machines use 193nm light. To print patterns smaller than that wavelength, you need tricks like multi-patterning (printing multiple times and overlapping). This works down to ~7nm but becomes impossibly complex and expensive beyond that. EUV's 13.5nm wavelength can print these features in a single pass ‚Äî it's not just better, it's necessary.
